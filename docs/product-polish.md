# Product Polish and Public Copy

Phase 8 makes CivicPulse AI understandable to a new visitor before they touch
the report form.

## Public promise

Use this as the primary product line:

> Report local problems. Verify with your community. Track until resolved.

Supporting copy should explain the loop plainly:

1. Citizens submit evidence.
2. Gemini structures the report for review.
3. The community verifies what is visible locally.
4. Statuses, notes, and timelines stay public.

## Copy boundaries

- Say that AI structures reports; do not say that AI makes official decisions.
- Say that community signals strengthen reports; do not say they prove legal or
  government acceptance.
- Say that administrators update statuses; do not imply every administrator is a
  government official.
- Keep reporter contact details out of public copy and public surfaces.
- Keep the government-affiliation disclaimer visible in the footer.

## Visual boundaries

- Prefer clear static sections over animation.
- Keep primary actions visible on desktop and mobile.
- Use the same badge, card, button, and typography system across public pages.
- Preserve keyboard focus states and avoid hiding controls behind hover-only
  behavior.
